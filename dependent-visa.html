Dependent Visa

A dependent visa is crucial as it allows family members or dependents of an individual holding a valid visa or residency in a particular country to accompany them and reside legally. This visa ensures the unity of families and provides essential support systems for individuals living and working abroad. It grants dependents access to education, healthcare, and other social services, developing a stable family environment.

Additionally, it enables spouses, children, or other dependents to participate in the cultural, social, and economic aspects of the host country, promoting integration and enhancing overall well-being.

Canada spouse visa

Choosing Canada for a spouse visa can be a compelling option for several reasons, with recent statistics reflecting a positive environment for applicants.

No points-based system

Unlike many other countries, Canada's spousal sponsorship program doesn't rely on a point system where applicants compete based on skills or education. As long as you meet the basic requirements and demonstrate a genuine relationship, you have a good chance of succeeding.

Relatively faster processing

Compared to other permanent residency options, processing times for spousal visas tend to be shorter, currently averaging around 12-18 months.

Online application option

Smooth online applications simplify the process, making it more convenient and accessible.

Immediate permanent residency

Upon approval, your spouse receives unconditional permanent residency, granting them the right to live, work, and study anywhere in Canada, unlike temporary visas with limited duration and restrictions.

Access to social services

Permanent residents have access to Canada's universal healthcare system, public education, and other social benefits.

Open work permit

Spouses can apply for an open work permit, allowing them to work in any occupation without sponsorship from an employer. This brings independence and career opportunities.

Why Choose Dependent UK Visa

  • Eligibility: Spouses, civil partners, or unmarried partners in long-term relationships (2+ years), dependent children under 18.
  • Visa types: Family Visitor visa for short stays, Tier 4 Dependant visa for longer stays linked to the student's visa.
  • Benefits: Live with your student in the UK, access healthcare and education (certain conditions apply).
  • Requirements: Proof of relationship, financial support, student's valid visa, meeting health and character requirements.
  • Application: Online, supporting documents needed (relationship evidence, finances, maintenance funds).
  • Processing time: Varies, can be several months.
