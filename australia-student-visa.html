Study in Australia

  • The best education in English: Australia boasts 7 universities in the 2024 QS World University Rankings Top 100, including the University of Melbourne at #2! This guarantees access to world-class education in a familiar language like English.
  • A welcoming environment: Australia consistently ranks high in surveys like the Expat Insider's annual survey, with over 88% of expats feeling welcome and included in the community. This makes adjusting to life as a student feel smoother and more positive.
  • Thriving job market for graduates: Australian employers value international skills and experience. A 2022 GradConnection report states that 71% of international student graduates secure employment within 3 months of graduation, showcasing strong career prospects.
  • Affordable compared to other top destinations: While costs can vary, living expenses and tuition fees in Australia are generally lower than in destinations like the UK or US, making it a financially attractive option for many students.
  • A diverse and adventurous lifestyle: Australia offers stunning natural beauty, vibrant cities, and a laid-back culture. From the Great Barrier Reef to cosmopolitan Melbourne, students can enjoy unique experiences and outdoor adventures alongside their studies.

Types of Student Visa

Student Visa (Subclass 500): For international students enrolled in a full-time course of study in Australia.

Student Guardian Visa (subclass 590): For individuals who need to provide care to a student under 18 years old.

Temporary Graduate Visa (Subclass 485): For recent graduates who have completed their studies in Australia and wish to stay temporarily to gain work experience.

Training Visa (Subclass 407): Purpose: For individuals participating in occupational training or professional development in Australia.

Eligibility:

  • Completion of an eligible qualification in Australia.
  • Meet Australian study and English language proficiency requirements.
  • Electronic Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) from an Australian education provider.
  • Proof of Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC).

Intake:

  • February and July intake.
  • Few universities are also offering minor takes, which is in October and November.
